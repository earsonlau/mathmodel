        What's challenging is trying to figure out the best foreign language to learn as an American. I have studied five different languages (French, Spanish, Arabic, Russian and Greek), and I would say Spanish is the most practical for Americans to learn. It does not require learning a new alphabet, and so many of the words have Latin equivalents in English. That said, I do think French is easier to learn cognitively than Spanish, as the word order is closer to English, and the way French speakers think and express themselves is really much closer to English-speaking cultures than they care to admit. If you can master reading in French, it's a wonderful, aesthetically captivating language to read; French writers are famous for a reason, and absorbing their thoughts will make you a romantic aesthete in a heartbeat.
